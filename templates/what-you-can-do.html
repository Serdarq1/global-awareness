If you experienced sexual violence

Safety first

If you are in immediate danger, call local emergency services. Get to a safe place if possible.

Medical & evidence

If safe, seek medical care. Consider a forensic exam where available. Evidence collection has time windows; ask local providers.

Support & options

Consider reaching out to a trusted person or a hotline. You are in control: choose what steps feel right to you.

Documentation

Save messages, photos, dates, locations if it feels safe. Decide later how you want to use them.

If you are supporting someone

Believe & validate

Say: “I’m sorry this happened. I believe you. It’s not your fault.”

Offer choices

Ask what they need. Offer options (medical, legal, counseling) without pressure.

Respect privacy

Keep information confidential unless there’s immediate danger or you have a duty to report.

Bystander skills (5 D’s)

Direct

Address the behavior if safe.

Distract

Create a diversion.

Delegate

Get help from staff or authorities.

Delay

Check in afterwards.

Document

Record details if safe and legal.

Workplace / School actions

  • Clear policies, reporting channels, and anti-retaliation protections.
  • Regular training on consent, boundaries, and bystander intervention.
  • Confidential support and accommodations for survivors.
